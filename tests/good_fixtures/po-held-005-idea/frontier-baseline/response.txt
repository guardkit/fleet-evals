The idea: a physiotherapy clinic wants self-service home exercise programmes that
patients follow between appointments, with the treating physio able to adjust
remotely. The brief is deliberately thin — platform, regulatory regime, depth of
booking-system integration, patient-data handling and the clinician's working
channel are all unstated. Idea-mode discipline: propose concrete features, state
my interpretation, and surface every unstated specific I rely on as a
confidence-tagged assumption or an open question rather than asserting it.

Core hypothesis: adherence between appointments is the clinic's real product
gap; the appointment itself already works. So the roadmap centres on the
programme lifecycle: author → follow → observe → adjust. Three bounded contexts
fall out naturally: Programme Authoring (clinician composes), Patient Experience
(patient follows and reports), Clinical Oversight (physio observes and adjusts).

Dependencies: the library precedes the composer; the composer precedes the
patient view; adherence capture precedes both the progress timeline and the
oversight dashboard; remote adjustment needs the composer (to edit) and the
dashboard (to know what to edit). Two specifics I genuinely rely on and must
license: read-only PMS lookups for patient/appointment identity, and opt-in push
notifications as the reminder channel. Regulatory regime I leave as an
assumption (UK GDPR posture) without baking it into any feature text.
